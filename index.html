ABOUT THE EVENT

BIOTHON 2026 is a national-level hackathon organized by the Department of Bioinformatics at Marwadi University, sponsored by the Royal Society of Biology and IEEE EMBS. Participants design and develop fully functional, visually stunning solutions across Biology, Healthcare, Agriculture, and Environment domains.

THE DOMAINS

HEALTH CARE

DOMAIN 1

5 challenges spanning AI diagnostics, wearable sensors, hospital management, digital mental health, and multilingual public health chatbots.

AGRICULTURE

DOMAIN 2

5 challenges covering AI crop disease detection, smart irrigation, direct farmer marketplaces, advisory systems, and precision farming.

BIODIVERSITY & ENVIRONMENT

DOMAIN 3

10 challenges across marine AI platforms, eDNA taxonomy, commodity price prediction, ocean data chatbots, deepfake detection, Ganga water forecasting, green hydrogen modelling, blockchain carbon registries, water footprint tracking, and MSW waste-to-energy.

HEALTH CARE

CHALLENGE 01

Wearable AI Sensor for Fall Prevention in Elderly

Design a wearable device with AI algorithms that detects when a senior's centre of gravity shifts dangerously, providing real-time tactile, audio, and visual alerts to prevent falls before they happen.

Wearable  |  AI/ML  |  Elderly Care  |  IMU Sensors

CHALLENGE 02

Digital Mental Health & Psychological Support System for Students

Create a digital platform offering mental health resources, mood tracking, anonymous counselling connections, and AI-based early warning for students in higher education facing psychological distress.

Mental Health  |  AI Screening  |  Students  |  Anonymous Platform

CHALLENGE 03

Non-Invasive Blood Vessel Blockage Detection

Develop a cost-effective, non-invasive technology to detect blockages in blood vessels without radioactive contrast agents making vascular diagnostics safer and more accessible in rural clinics.

BioTech  |  Medical Device  |  Non-invasive  |  Diagnostics

CHALLENGE 04

Queuing & Bed Management System for Hospital OPDs

A smart hospital management solution integrating queuing models for OPDs, real-time bed availability, patient admission tracking, and medicine inventory reducing wait times and improving workflow.

Hospital Management  |  Real-time  |  Dashboard  |  Queue Optimisation

CHALLENGE 05

AI-Driven Public Health Chatbot for Disease Awareness

Build a conversational AI chatbot that educates citizens about disease prevention, symptoms, and nearest healthcare resources deployable in regional languages for rural populations.

NLP Chatbot  |  Multilingual  |  Public Health

CHALLENGE 06

Open Innovation

Have a groundbreaking idea in Health Care? Present your innovative solution or prototype that addresses a critical challenge not covered by the other problem statements.

Open Idea  |  Innovation  |  Health Care

AGRICULTURE

CHALLENGE 01

Farmers Disease Diagnostic / Reporting Portal

An AI-powered mobile portal where farmers photograph disease symptoms on crops or livestock, receive AI diagnosis and suggested treatments, and alerts are sent to the nearest veterinarian or agri expert.

AI Diagnosis  |  Image Recognition  |  Mobile  |  Alert System

CHALLENGE 02

Smart Irrigation System for Precision Farming

Design an IoT + sensor-based system to monitor real-time soil moisture and weather, then auto-schedule irrigation reducing water wastage and maximising crop yield through a mobile dashboard.

IoT  |  Water Conservation  |  Sensors  |  Dashboard

CHALLENGE 03

Mobile / Web App for Direct Market Access for Farmers

Connect farmers directly with consumers and retailers through a marketplace app eliminating middlemen, enabling price negotiation, produce listing, and secure transaction management.

Marketplace  |  Agri Commerce  |  Mobile

CHALLENGE 04

Smart Crop Advisory System for Small & Marginal Farmers

Create an advisory platform that recommends crop types, planting schedules, and practices tailored to small farmers' land size, soil data, and local weather bridging the knowledge gap in rural India.

Advisory System  |  Farmer App  |  Data Analytics

CHALLENGE 05

AI-Driven Crop Disease Prediction & Management System

Develop an AI system that analyses crop images and environmental data to predict disease outbreaks early, giving farmers actionable treatment recommendations via a mobile or web app.

AI/ML  |  Computer Vision  |  Web App  |  Real-time Data

CHALLENGE 06

Open Innovation

Have a groundbreaking idea in Agriculture? Present your innovative solution or prototype that addresses a critical challenge not covered by the other problem statements.

Open Idea  |  Innovation  |  Agriculture

BIODIVERSITY & ENVIRONMENT

CHALLENGE 01

AI-Driven Unified Data Platform for Oceanographic & Fisheries Insights

Build a unified AI platform integrating oceanographic datasets, fisheries records and molecular biodiversity data providing predictive insights on fish migration, marine health and sustainable fishing zones.

AI Platform  |  Big Data  |  Geospatial ML  |  Marine Science

CHALLENGE 02

Identifying Taxonomy and Assessing Biodiversity from eDNA Datasets

Use environmental DNA (eDNA) sequencing data to identify species taxonomy and assess biodiversity in aquatic ecosystems a bioinformatics pipeline combining sequence alignment, ML classification and ecological reporting.

Bioinformatics  |  eDNA  |  ML Classification  |  Biodiversity

CHALLENGE 03

AI/ML Models for Predicting Prices of Agricultural Commodities

Develop predictive models for forecasting prices of pulses and vegetables (onion, potato, tomato) using historical trends, seasonality, market intelligence and satellite crop data powering smarter government interventions.

Time-series ML  |  ARIMA  |  Price Prediction  |  Data Science

CHALLENGE 04

FloatChat AI Conversational Interface for Ocean Data Discovery

Create a natural language chatbot interface that lets researchers and citizens query and visualise oceanographic data from ARGO floats making complex scientific datasets accessible through plain-English conversation.

LLM  |  Data Visualisation  |  Science Portal

CHALLENGE 05

AI/ML Solution for Detecting Face-Swap Deepfake Videos

Build a deep learning model that detects face-swap deepfake videos by analysing spatial, temporal and frequency anomalies outputting a detailed forensic report on whether a video is authentic or manipulated.

Deep Learning  |  CNN/RNN  |  Computer Vision  |  Cybersecurity

CHALLENGE 06

Real-time Ganga River Water Quality Forecasting using AI and IoT

Build an AI-enabled Decision Support System integrating satellite data, IoT sensors and hydrological models to forecast Ganga water quality (DO, BOD, pathogens) 3–5 days ahead with public mobile dashboard and alerts.

Satellite Data  |  IoT  |  Water Quality  |  AI/DSS

CHALLENGE 07

Industrial-Scale Green Hydrogen Production Facility Concept

Design a detailed concept for a green hydrogen facility producing 50+ tonnes/day at under $2/kg levelised cost including electrolyser sizing, renewable energy model, CAPEX/OPEX breakdown and carbon credit estimation.

Green Energy  |  Techno-Economics  |  Hydrogen  |  Modelling

CHALLENGE 08

Blockchain-Based Blue Carbon Registry & MRV System

Create a blockchain-based registry to measure, report and verify (MRV) blue carbon stored in coastal ecosystems (mangroves, seagrass) enabling transparent carbon credit issuance and climate finance tracking.

Blockchain  |  Carbon Credits  |  Climate Tech  |  Coastal Ecology

CHALLENGE 09

Digital Water Footprint Calculator for Agricultural Products

Develop a user-friendly web/mobile app where users scan or enter food items to instantly learn their water footprint driving awareness about virtual water consumption in agriculture and promoting conservation behaviour.

Sustainability App  |  AI Scan  |  Water Conservation  |  Education

CHALLENGE 10

Waste to Energy Innovative MSW Recycling and Community Solution

Design a community-based digital tracking and management system for municipal solid waste from segregation at source through collection, recycling and conversion to bioenergy, with landfill gas monitoring.

Waste Management  |  IoT  |  Tracking  |  Dashboard

CHALLENGE 11

Open Innovation

Have a groundbreaking idea in Biodiversity & Environment? Present your innovative solution or prototype that addresses a critical challenge not covered by the other problem statements.

Open Idea  |  Innovation  |  Biodiversity & Environment

RULES

ROUND 01

PPT Submission

1st June 2026

Teams submit their problem understanding, proposed solution, technology stack, and expected outcomes through the official presentation template for evaluation.

ROUND 02

Video Submission

20th June 2026

Shortlisted teams submit a detailed walkthrough video explaining their solution, innovation, implementation approach, and team understanding.

ROUND 03

Grand Finale (Offline)

4th July 2026

Marwadi University, Rajkot, Gujarat

Finalist teams present their live working prototype, solution pitch, and implementation before an expert judging panel during the offline finale event.

  • Free Registration
  • Complimentary Meals for Finalists
  • Night Stay Available (Outstation Teams)
  • Open Innovation is allowed, but it must align with one of the chosen domains: Health Care, Agriculture, or Biodiversity & Environment.
  • Open to all developers, designers, and students teams of 2–4 members.
  • All participants must be currently enrolled college students.
  • Inter-college and inter-specialization teams are allowed.
  • One member must be designated as the Team Lead for all communication.
  • A participant cannot register in more than one team.
  • Team composition cannot be changed after registration is confirmed.
  • Round 1: Submit your solution concept using the official BIOTHON 2026 template in PDF format before the deadline.
  • Round 2: Selected teams must present a fully functional, working solution to the judges in-person at Marwadi University.
  • All team members must be present on the day of Round 2.
  • Late submissions will not be accepted under any circumstances.
  • AI tools are officially allowed for design, ideation, and development.
  • All submitted work must be 100% original plagiarism will result in immediate disqualification.
  • Judges' decisions are final and binding.
  • Respectful behaviour toward all judges, organizers, and participants is mandatory at all times.
